Launch and Landing Timeline

NASA's Mars rover program has evolved over decades, with each mission building on the discoveries and lessons of its predecessors. The timeline below highlights key milestones for Sojourner, Curiosity, and Perseverance.

Key Mission Milestones

1996–1997

Sojourner – Mars Pathfinder

Launch: December 1996 · Landing: July 1997 in Ares Vallis.
Sojourner became the first rover to roll across the Martian surface, testing mobility, imaging, and basic rock analysis.

2011–2012

Curiosity – Mars Science Laboratory

Launch: November 2011 from Cape Canaveral · Landing: August 2012 in Gale Crater.
Curiosity's dramatic "seven minutes of terror" entry, descent, and landing showcased the sky-crane system and opened a new era of large, lab-equipped rovers.

2020–2021

Perseverance – Mars 2020

Launch: July 2020 on an Atlas V · Landing: February 2021 in Jezero Crater.
Perseverance carried the Ingenuity helicopter and the most advanced sample caching system yet, targeting an ancient lakebed for signs of past life.

Major Discoveries and Accomplishments

Curiosity in Gale Crater

  • Found evidence of ancient streams and lakes within Gale Crater.
  • Detected organic molecules preserved in Martian rocks.
  • Measured radiation levels relevant to future human explorers.

Perseverance in Jezero Crater

  • Investigated rocks that likely formed in an ancient river delta.
  • Begun collecting and sealing samples for future return missions.
  • Supported dozens of successful flights of the Ingenuity helicopter.

Sojourner’s Pathfinder

  • Demonstrated basic rover driving and obstacle avoidance on Mars.
  • Sent back close-up images of Martian rocks and soil.
  • Proved that mobile robots could dramatically expand surface science.
